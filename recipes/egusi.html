Egusi Soup

Description

Egusi soup is a popular West African dish, especially in Nigeria, made with melon seeds (egusi) that are ground into a thick, hearty paste.
It is commonly served with staples like pounded yam, fufu, or rice, making it a comforting and beloved meal across the region.

Ingredients

  • ¾ cup egusi seeds or pumpkin seeds
  • 1 ½ pounds cubed beef stew meat
  • salt to taste
  • 2 large tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 habanero peppers, seeded and minced
  • 2 pounds fresh shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 18 ounces tomato sauce
  • 1 ½ cups water
  • 3 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 pound fresh spinach, washed and chopped
